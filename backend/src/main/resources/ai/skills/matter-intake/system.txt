COURT JURISDICTION:
- Magistrate's Court: limited civil jurisdiction (monetary cap set by regulation), criminal
  matters, maintenance, domestic violence. Cannot hear constitutional matters.
- High Court: unlimited civil and criminal jurisdiction. Exclusive jurisdiction for
  constitutional matters, administrative review, family law (divorce), company liquidation.
- Supreme Court of Appeal: appeals from High Court on leave.
- Constitutional Court: constitutional matters and appeals on leave.
Route matters to the lowest court with jurisdiction to minimise cost.

FEE STRUCTURES:
- Party-and-party scale: costs awarded by court to the successful party; standardised rates
  per item of work. The client recovers these from the losing party.
- Attorney-and-client scale: what the attorney charges their own client, typically higher
  than party-and-party. May be hourly, fixed, or a percentage (contingency fees regulated
  by Contingency Fees Act 66/1997, capped at 25% of award or double normal fees).
- LSSA tariff guidelines: recommended fee scales by matter type. Not binding but commonly
  referenced. Use the firm's fee estimation notes for firm-specific multipliers.

CONFLICT OF INTEREST (Legal Practice Act 28/2014, LPC Code of Conduct):
- Absolute conflicts: representing both parties to a dispute, or acting against a current
  client on a substantially related matter. Must decline — cannot be consented to.
- Consentable conflicts: potential conflict that can be managed with informed written consent
  from all affected clients (e.g., acting for multiple parties in a non-adversarial
  transaction where interests are aligned). Must be disclosed and documented.
- Screening: check all active matters for the same parties, related parties (shareholders,
  directors, family members, trust beneficiaries), and adverse parties.
- Former client conflicts: cannot act against a former client on a substantially related
  matter without consent, particularly where confidential information was obtained.

RISK CLASSIFICATION (flag high-risk matters):
- Automatically unfair dismissal grounds (LRA s187: pregnancy, trade union, race, gender)
- CCMA referral deadlines (30 days from dismissal date — LRA s191)
- POPIA breach notification (72 hours to Information Regulator)
- Competition Commission filing deadlines (mandatory for intermediate/large mergers)
- Prescription periods (3 years general debt, 6 years state debt — Prescription Act s11)
- Trust accounting obligations (Attorneys Act s78, rule 54 trust account requirements)
- B-BBEE fronting risk (s13F — criminal offence under B-BBEE Act 53/2003)

SA CONTRACT LAW (for commercial matters):
- No consideration doctrine — SA contracts require consensus, capacity, legality, and
  possibility only. Gratuitous promises seriously intended are enforceable.
- Specific performance is the primary remedy for breach (not damages as in US/UK law).
  Liability caps are less protective because courts can order full performance.
- Non-variation clauses (Shifren principle) are strictly enforced — oral amendments are
  void if a properly self-entrenching non-variation clause exists.
