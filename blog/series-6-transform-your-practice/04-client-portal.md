# Your Clients Can Help Themselves (And They Prefer It)

*Part 4 of "Run a Leaner Practice with Kazi."*

---

Count the emails you sent last week asking clients for documents.

"Hi John, can you please send the bank statements for January to March?" "Hi Sarah, the certified ID we have on file has expired — could you send an updated one?" "Hi Mr. Naidoo, we need the fixed asset register before we can complete the year-end — could you forward it?"

Each email takes 2 minutes to write. Then there's the follow-up when they don't respond. Then the second follow-up. Then the phone call. Then they send the wrong document. Then you clarify. Then they resend.

For a firm with 40 clients, document chasing is a part-time job nobody applied for.

## The Portal: Your Clients' Own Space

Kazi gives each client their own portal — a simple, password-free website where they can see their engagements, view shared documents, respond to requests, and accept proposals.

No app to install. No account to create. The client clicks a link in an email (a secure magic link — unique to them, time-limited), and they're in. They see their firm's logo, their engagement list, and whatever you've shared with them.

### What Clients Can See

**Engagements**: The client sees their active engagements — "Monthly Bookkeeping," "Annual Tax Return FY2026," "VAT Returns Q1" — with status and progress.

**Documents**: Any document you've shared with them. Engagement letters, tax certificates, financial statements, compliance reports. They can view and download — not edit or upload (unless responding to a request).

**Comments**: A conversation thread on each engagement. The client posts a question — "When should I expect the Q1 VAT return?" — and your team responds. All in one place, timestamped, attached to the relevant engagement. Not buried in an email chain.

### What Clients Can Do

**Respond to information requests**: Instead of emailing "please send bank statements," you create an Information Request in Kazi:

```
Request: Year-End Supporting Documents
Items:
  ☐ Trial Balance (PDF or Excel)
  ☐ Bank Statements — full year (PDF)
  ☐ Loan Agreements (PDF)
  ☐ Fixed Asset Register (PDF or Excel)
  ☐ Payroll Summary — EMP201 (PDF or Excel)
  ☐ Debtors Age Analysis (PDF or Excel, optional)
```

The client sees this in their portal as a structured checklist. They upload each document against the specific item. You see the progress in real time — 4 of 6 items submitted.

No "which email had the attachment?" No "did they send the right version?" Each document is uploaded to a specific item with a timestamp and file name.

**Accept proposals and engagement letters**: When you send an engagement letter, the client receives a link to view the document and type their name to accept. Digital acceptance with a timestamp, IP address, and a Certificate of Acceptance generated automatically. No printing, no scanning, no couriering.

### What Clients Cannot Do

Clients can't see your internal notes, time entries, cost rates, margin calculations, or team communications. They see what you choose to share — nothing more.

## Why Magic Links, Not Passwords

Your clients already have too many passwords. Adding another one for your firm's portal creates friction — "I forgot my password" becomes your support problem.

Kazi uses **magic links**: the client clicks a link in an email, and they're authenticated. The link is:
- **Unique** to that client
- **Time-limited** (expires after 24 hours)
- **Single-use** for authentication (can be re-sent anytime)

The client doesn't create an account. They don't choose a password. They don't install an authenticator app. They click a link. If they need access again next week, they request a new link — or you send one from the client's page in Kazi.

For the 60-year-old trust beneficiary who barely uses email, this is the difference between "I'll try to log in later" and actually accessing the document you need them to review.

## The Email Volume Effect

A firm with 40 clients and 4 information requests per client per year sends ~160 request emails. Each request averages 3-4 back-and-forth messages (initial request, clarification, follow-up, confirmation). That's 500-640 emails per year — just for document collection.

With Kazi's portal:
- **Initial request**: Created in Kazi, client notified via single email with portal link
- **Clarification**: Client reads the item description in the portal (no separate email)
- **Follow-up**: Kazi shows progress; you nudge only incomplete items
- **Confirmation**: Automatic when all items are submitted

The email volume drops from 500+ to ~160 (one notification per request). The rest happens in the portal — structured, tracked, and attached to the right engagement.

## Comments: One Thread, Both Sides

The comment system deserves special mention because it solves a subtle problem: where does the client conversation live?

In most firms, it's in email. The partner's inbox has the strategic discussions. The admin's inbox has the document requests. The article clerk's inbox has the technical questions. Nobody has the full picture.

In Kazi, comments are attached to the engagement. Both your team and the client post to the same thread. The team sees everything. The client sees everything that's shared with them. No forwarding, no CC chains, no "did you see John's email about the trust amendment?"

When the partner needs context before a client call, they open the engagement and scroll the comment thread. The full history — questions, responses, decisions — is right there.

## The Trust Factor

Some firms worry that a client portal feels impersonal. "Our clients value the personal relationship — they call us, we call them."

The portal doesn't replace phone calls. It replaces the administrative follow-up that nobody enjoys:

- Instead of "I'll email you the tax return" → share it in the portal, send a link
- Instead of "Can you send the bank statements?" → create a structured request with clear items
- Instead of "Where's the engagement letter I sent last month?" → it's in the portal, with the acceptance record

The personal relationship is the strategic advice, the year-end planning session, the "here's how to save R50,000 in tax" conversation. The portal handles the paperwork so you have more time for the relationship.

## Getting Started

You don't have to move all clients to the portal on day one. Start with:

1. **Your most active clients** — the ones who generate the most document exchange
2. **One information request** — year-end documents or a quarterly VAT request
3. **Observe the response** — most clients prefer the portal once they've used it once

The clients who like it will start asking: "Can I see my invoices in here too?" That's your signal to share more.
