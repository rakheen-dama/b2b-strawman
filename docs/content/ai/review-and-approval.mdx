# AI Review & Approval

HeyKazi's AI never changes a record on its own. When an [AI Specialist](/ai/specialists) recommends a
consequential action — marking a FICA checklist complete, applying a recommendation — it creates an
**approval gate**. The action only happens once a person with review authority approves it. This keeps
the liability surface where it belongs: with the attorney, not the model.

## The approval queue

Gates appear in the **AI Reviews** queue (Settings → AI → Reviews). Reviewing requires the `AI_REVIEW`
capability — typically owners, admins, and supervising attorneys.

Each gate shows:

- **What the AI proposes** — the specific change (e.g. which checklist items to mark complete).
- **The AI's reasoning** — why it recommends the action.
- **Where it came from** — the specialist run that produced it.
- **An expiry** — gates lapse if not actioned (default **72 hours**).

## Approving or rejecting

<Steps>
### Open a pending gate

Go to the **Reviews** queue and open a gate in the **Pending** tab.

### Review the proposal and reasoning

Read what the AI wants to do and why. Open the underlying client, matter, or document if you need
context.

### Approve or reject

- **Approve** — the proposed action is applied (for example, the checklist items are marked complete).
  You can add notes.
- **Reject** — nothing changes. Add a note explaining why; the proposal is discarded.
</Steps>

<Callout type="info">
  Approving a gate is what *executes* the change. Rejecting leaves your data exactly as it was. Either
  way, the decision — who, when, and any notes — is recorded.
</Callout>

## Expiry

A gate that is neither approved nor rejected within its window (72 hours by default) is automatically
marked **expired** and its action is never applied. Expired gates remain visible in the review history.

## Audit trail

Every step is written to the [audit log](/admin/org-settings): the specialist invocation
(`ai.specialist.invoked`), and the disposition (`ai.specialist.approved`, `ai.specialist.rejected`, or
`ai.specialist.expired`) with the reviewing member and timestamp. This gives you a defensible,
POPIA-friendly record of every AI-suggested change and who signed off on it.
