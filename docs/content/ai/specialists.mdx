# AI Specialists

AI Specialists are focused, one-shot skills for specific legal tasks. Unlike the conversational
[AI Assistant](/features/ai-assistant), a specialist takes a defined input (a client, a document, a
matter), runs once, and returns **structured, cited output** — and where it proposes a consequential
change, it creates an item in the [approval queue](/ai/review-and-approval) for an attorney to sign off.

Every specialist is grounded in your [AI firm profile](/ai/firm-profile-and-cost) (practice areas,
house style, risk posture) and South African legal knowledge, and runs on your organisation's own
Anthropic key (see [Integrations](/admin/integrations)). Triggering a specialist requires the
`AI_EXECUTE` capability.

## The specialists

### FICA Verification

Reviews a client's FICA/KYC file — their onboarding checklist and uploaded documents — and reports what
is complete, what is missing, and the risk level. For a trust or company client it accounts for
beneficial-ownership requirements.

- **Trigger:** a client's detail page.
- **Output:** overall assessment (complete / incomplete / needs review), risk level, a per-item
  checklist review, missing documents, and recommended actions.
- **Gate:** proposing to mark checklist items complete creates a **Mark KYC Complete** approval gate —
  the items are only marked once an attorney approves.

See also [FICA Verification](/features/fica-verification) for the underlying checklist workflow.

### Matter Intake

Triages a prospective matter from a short description: recommends the matter type and template,
estimates a fee on the applicable tariff basis, screens existing clients and matters for conflicts, and
flags early risks (prescription, deadlines).

- **Trigger:** a client's detail page, with a description of the new matter.
- **Output:** matter classification, template recommendation, required documents, a fee estimate (ZAR
  range), conflict screening result, and risk flags.

### Contract Review

Reads an uploaded agreement and produces a clause-level review against your risk posture, with South
African statutory references where relevant.

- **Trigger:** a document's detail page.
- **Output:** document classification, an executive summary, findings (severity, clause reference,
  statutory reference, recommendation), and missing protections.

### Drafting

Generates a first draft from a template and a matter's context — filling variables from real data and
producing narrative sections in your house style.

- **Trigger:** a template / document page on a matter.
- **Output:** variable fills (with source and confidence), narrative sections, clause recommendations,
  and warnings where information is missing.

### Compliance Audit

Runs a firm-wide compliance review across your matters, clients, documents, and trust accounting, and
grades it.

- **Trigger:** the organisation's compliance settings.
- **Output:** an overall grade, category scores, findings (with regulatory basis), and prioritised
  recommendations — surfacing issues like trust shortfalls or outstanding FICA items.

<Callout type="warning">
  Every specialist output is a **draft for attorney review**. Specialists never change a record on their
  own — anything consequential waits in the [approval queue](/ai/review-and-approval).
</Callout>

## What you'll see

When you run a specialist, HeyKazi shows the structured result, the model used, the run duration, and
the **cost in ZAR** (metered against your [monthly budget](/ai/firm-profile-and-cost)). Any approval
gates the specialist created are listed with their reasoning, ready for review.

## Limitations

- **Requires BYOAK** — a specialist will not run until an admin configures an Anthropic API key in
  [Integrations](/admin/integrations).
- **Document-dependent skills need documents** — Contract Review and document-based FICA review need the
  relevant file uploaded to the matter or client first.
- **Budget-capped** — if your organisation's monthly AI budget is reached, specialists are blocked until
  the budget is raised.
