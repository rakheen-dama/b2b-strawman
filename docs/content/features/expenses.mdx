# Expenses

Expenses capture the out-of-pocket costs your team incurs while working on a project — filing fees, travel, courier charges, software licences, and more. They appear on the **Expenses** tab of a project detail page alongside your time entries and other project data.

Tracking expenses separately from time ensures your invoices reflect the full cost of an engagement. Billable expenses can be included on invoices with optional markup, while non-billable expenses are recorded for internal cost visibility without being charged to the client.

## Key Concepts

### Expense Categories

When logging an expense, you choose a category that describes the type of cost.

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| **Filing Fee** | Court filing fees, registration costs, regulatory submissions |
| **Travel** | Flights, accommodation, mileage, parking |
| **Courier** | Document delivery, express shipping |
| **Software** | Licence fees, SaaS subscriptions, tools purchased for a project |
| **Subcontractor** | Third-party professional services, outsourced work |
| **Printing** | Document printing, binding, physical copies |
| **Communication** | Phone calls, postage, certified mail |
| **Other** | Any cost that does not fit the above categories |

### Billing Statuses

Each expense has a billing status that determines whether it appears on invoices.

| Status | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Unbilled** | A billable expense that has not yet been included on an invoice. |
| **Billed** | Included on an invoice. Billed expenses are locked and cannot be edited or deleted. |
| **Non-billable** | Marked as non-billable, either when logged or after being written off by an admin. Not included on invoices. |

### Billable Amount and Markup

When you set a markup percentage on an expense, the billable amount is calculated as the base amount multiplied by one plus the markup (for example, a R500 expense with 15% markup becomes R575 on the invoice). The billable amount appears as a separate column in the expense list so you can see both the actual cost and the client-facing charge.

## Logging an Expense

<Steps>
### Step 1 — Navigate to the Expenses tab

Open a project detail page and click the **Expenses** tab.

### Step 2 — Click Log Expense

Click the **Log Expense** button to open the expense dialog.

### Step 3 — Fill in the details

Complete the form fields:

- **Date** (required) — defaults to today
- **Description** (required) — a clear summary such as "Court filing fee" or "Client site visit — Johannesburg"
- **Amount** (required) — the actual cost incurred
- **Currency** (required) — defaults to your organisation's currency (for example, ZAR)
- **Category** (required) — select from the list of expense categories
- **Task** (optional) — link the expense to a specific task in the project
- **Markup %** (optional) — percentage markup applied when billing the client
- **Billable** (checkbox, on by default) — uncheck to record as a non-billable expense
- **Notes** (optional) — additional context or justification
- **Receipt** (optional) — upload an image, PDF, or document as supporting evidence

### Step 4 — Save the expense

Click **Save**. The expense appears in the list with an **Unbilled** status (or **Non-billable** if you unchecked the billable flag).
</Steps>

## Including Expenses on Invoices

When generating an invoice for a project, unbilled expenses appear alongside unbilled time entries as selectable line items. Each expense becomes an **Expense** line item on the invoice, using the billable amount (with markup applied, if any). Once an invoice is finalised, the included expenses move to **Billed** status and are locked from further editing.

## Write-off and Restore

Admins can write off an unbilled expense to move it to **Non-billable** status. This is useful when a cost was initially flagged as billable but you decide not to charge the client. If a write-off was made in error, admins can restore the expense back to **Unbilled** status.

<Callout type="warning">
  Billed expenses cannot be written off, edited, or deleted. To correct a billed expense, you need to adjust the invoice it appears on.
</Callout>

## Filtering and Reporting

The expense list supports filters to help you find what you need:

- **Billing status** — All, Unbilled, Billed, or Non-billable
- **Category** — filter by expense category
- **Member** — filter by the team member who logged the expense

These filters combine to give you a clear view of specific cost types, outstanding billable amounts, or individual team member spending.

## Tips and Best Practices

- **Attach receipts at the time of logging** — uploading supporting documents immediately avoids the scramble at invoicing time.
- **Use markup consistently** — if your firm marks up disbursements, set a standard percentage so clients see predictable charges.
- **Review unbilled expenses before invoicing** — check the expense list for any costs that should be written off or recategorised before generating an invoice.
- **Link expenses to tasks** — connecting an expense to a specific task gives you granular cost tracking and makes it easier to explain charges to clients.
- **Use categories for reporting** — consistent categorisation helps you spot spending patterns across projects and identify cost savings.
